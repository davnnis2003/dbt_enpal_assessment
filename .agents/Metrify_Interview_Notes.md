# Metrify Interview Notes & Context

This document compiles notes, transcripts, and context from the recruiter screening and hiring manager interviews for the **Data Engineering Lead / Analytics Engineering Lead** role at Metrify (a startup inside Enpal).

---

## 1. Role Context & Team Setup

### About Metrify
- **Business**: A "startup inside a startup" at Enpal. Small, young, motivated, but currently lacking supervision and structure.
- **Office**: Hybrid setup (expecting 3–4 days/week in office). Located at the Empire campus (Berlin), allowing knowledge sharing with the larger Enpal central data team.
- **Current Data Team**: Small team of ~4 people:
  - 1 Data Engineer
  - 2 Analytics Engineers
  - 1 Technical Project Manager (PM)
- **Hiring Manager**: Marcus, the CTO (currently on parental leave, but involved in final interview stages).

### Core Challenges & Priorities
- **Flat & Decentralized structure**: Currently has fuzzy reporting lines, lack of centralized data vision, and weak coordination.
- **No Motivation Issues, But Lack of Guidance**: The team wants to deliver but needs structure, best practices, and close guidance.
- **Greenfield Environment**: A key requirement is bringing structure to chaos and centralizing the data model/infrastructure.
- **Cross-team Collaboration**: There has been friction between Metrify and the central energy team (e.g., energy finance team needing smart meter / 15-minute customer energy readings from Metrify, facing response/delivery bottlenecks).

## 2. Jimmy Pang's Background & Interview Alignment

### Experience Profile
- **Career Trajectory**: Progressed from intern to senior and engineering manager. Extensive hands-on experience in early phases, moving to managerial/leadership roles later.
- **Key Achievements Mentioned**:
  - **Transportation Data Table Rebuild**: Rebuilt a chaotic transportation logic/table into a robust, microservices & Kafka-based pipeline. Became the 5th most used table company-wide and earned praise for reliability and trustworthiness.
  - **Data Mesh Pilot**: Led a data mesh pilot, adapting the federal/decentralized concept to fit a more centralized/coordinated organizational culture.
- **Tech Stack Familiarity**: DBT, Snowflake, Tableau, Airflow, Kafka, Postgres, microservices.

### Proposed 30/60/90 Day Plan
- **Day 1 - 30 (Handshake & Trust)**:
  - Focus heavily on relationship building and stakeholder mapping.
  - Gather a "wish list" of requests and identify current pain points.
  - Identify and deliver quick wins to establish credibility and trust.
- **Day 30 - 60 (Process & Prioritization)**:
  - Establish a single source of intake/prioritization for stakeholder requests to eliminate silos.
  - Introduce agile methodologies (e.g., Scrum/Kanban) to improve expectation management and team predictability.
  - Measure baseline team performance (e.g., lead time from ticket creation to resolution).
- **Day 60 - 90 (Solidification & Feedback)**:
  - Solidify delivery processes, perform retrospectives with stakeholders, and pivot processes based on feedback.

## 3. Interview Takeaways & Alignment Points

- **Positioning**: Hands-on data leader who brings structure to messy systems, bridges the business-tech gap, and thrives in a greenfield startup environment.
- **Ideal Fit**: A player-coach role (retaining significant hands-on technical involvement, especially in the early stages, while managing and guiding the team).
- **Stance on AI**: Views AI as a productivity booster. Promotes stable semantic/semantic modeling layers as the foundation so that AI/BI tools can dynamically serve operational insights without breaking.
- **Deal Breaker**: Lack of curiosity is non-negotiable.
